World Cup Travel Tips
Plan smart. Watch more football.
The World Cup across three countries requires more planning than a typical trip. Here's what you need to know.
🏨
Book hotels immediately
Match-day hotels near stadiums are already selling out for key fixtures. New York Final dates and LA opener are nearly gone. Book now and cancel later if plans change — most hotels offer free cancellation.
🎫
Tickets — official channels only
Official tickets via FIFA.com only. Resale is legal but expensive — hospitality packages available through On Location. Brazil, Argentina and USA matches sell out fastest. Book your city's tourist activities for matchless days.
✈️
Multi-city routing
Many fans are planning multi-city trips. Open-jaw flights (fly into one city, out of another) work well. USA domestic flights are fast — LA to Dallas is 3 hours. Mexico City and Toronto add easy international variety.
🌡️
June–July weather varies
Houston and Miami: very hot and humid (35°C+). Dallas: hot (38°C). Los Angeles and San Francisco: mild and pleasant (22–26°C). Vancouver and Seattle: cooler (18–22°C). Canada is the best weather escape if heat is an issue.
🚇
Getting to stadiums
Most US stadiums have limited public transport — Uber/Lyft surge prices massively on match days. Book shuttle services in advance where available. New York's MetLife Stadium has NJ Transit rail — the easiest stadium commute of all.
💰
Budget reality check
World Cup travel is expensive. Hotel prices near stadiums on match days are 3–5x normal. Book non-match nights at the same hotel for much lower rates. Fan zones are free and a great alternative experience for matchless days.
