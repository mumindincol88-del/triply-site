🌸 Turkey Spring Festivals 2026

Two festivals, one spring — Alaçatı Herb Festival & Manisa Mesir Macunu

📅 April 2026 📍 İzmir & Manisa, Turkey ✍️ First-hand guide by Triply ⏱ 7 min read
🌿 Alaçatı Herb Festival 🎊 Manisa Mesir Macunu 🎨 Frida Kahlo Exhibition 📅 Planning tips

April 2026 brought two of Turkey's most spectacular spring festivals within days of each other. I went to both — Alaçatı's wild herb celebration in the Aegean and Manisa's 486-year-old Mesir Macunu tradition. Here's what you actually need to know.

01
Festival One · İzmir
Alaçatı Herb Festival (Ot Festivali)
📅 April 23–26, 2026 · 📍 Alaçatı, Çeşme
"Köklerden Dünyaya" — 15th edition

What is the Alaçatı Herb Festival?

The Alaçatı Herb Festival (Ot Festivali) is held every April in the stone-paved town of Alaçatı, just outside Çeşme on the Aegean coast. It celebrates the wild herb and edible plant culture of the Aegean region — a tradition that goes back generations, now grown into a proper festival with food, crafts and live atmosphere.

The 2026 edition opened on April 23rd with a colourful cortège parade, which coincided with Turkey's National Sovereignty and Children's Day. The combination made the opening day particularly charged — people in vibrant traditional costumes filling the streets, brass bands, dancing groups, the whole thing.

💡 First-hand tip — Opening day is special

April 23rd combines the festival opening with a national holiday. The cortège on this day has a different energy to the rest of the festival — more emotional, more festive. If you can only go one day, go on the 23rd and arrive early to get a spot on the main street.

The three zones — what's where

The festival is laid out across three distinct areas. Most people gravitate to the food zone and miss the herb market — which is actually the most interesting part.

01

🌿 Herb Market

Wild Aegean herbs, edible plants, fresh produce. The soul of the festival — vendors who forage and grow what they sell.

02

🍽️ Food Area

Traditional Aegean cooking — dishes made with the exact herbs from Zone 1. Otlu börek, stuffed flowers, artichoke wraps.

03

🎨 Crafts & Gifts

Handmade ceramics, textiles, local art. Quality noticeably higher than typical tourist markets.

Zone 1 — The herb market

This is what makes Alaçatı's festival different from any other food market. The herbs here are wild-foraged or locally grown — many varieties you won't find anywhere else. Vendors know their plants and are happy to explain uses and recipes.

What you'll find:

🌿 Enginar (Artichoke) 🌾 Arap Saçı 🍋 Çeşme Limonu 🌱 Kuzu Limonu 🫚 Limon Kekiği 🌿 Üzerlik Otu 🌾 Tembel Avrat Otu 🌱 Şevketi Bostan 🍃 Koruk 🌿 Çiriş Otu 🌱 Vibes Otu Kulağı 🥬 Kuşkonmaz
💡 What to buy

Çeşme Limonu is rare outside this region — buy it if you see it. Kuşkonmaz (asparagus) from the Aegean is exceptional in spring. Ask vendors how to use what you're buying — they'll tell you the recipe and why it grows here.

Zone 2 — Aegean food

The food zone puts the herbs to work. Everything is made with local and foraged ingredients — this is proper Aegean cooking, not festival fast food.

🥬 Otlu börekHerb-filled pastry — a staple
🌸 Kabak çiçeği dolmasıStuffed zucchini blossoms

⚠️ Time it right

The food zone is very busy 12:00–14:00. Go at 11:00 to eat without waiting, or after 15:00 when the lunch crowd clears. Portions are generous and prices are fair.

After the festival — Alaçatı town & Ilıca beach

The festival takes half a day maximum. Spend the afternoon wandering Alaçatı's stone streets — boutique shops, bougainvillea, café terraces. It's one of the most photogenic towns on the Aegean coast.

End the day at Ilıca, just a few kilometres away. Shallow turquoise water, calm atmosphere, one of the best beach experiences close to İzmir. A perfect close to a festival day.

02
Festival Two · Manisa — UNESCO Heritage
486th Manisa Mesir Macunu Festival
📅 April 2026 · 📍 Sultan Camisi, Manisa
🏛️ UNESCO Intangible Heritage

What is Mesir Macunu?

📜 486 years of history

The story goes back to the 16th century Ottoman court. Hafsa Sultan — mother of Süleyman the Magnificent, wife of Yavuz Sultan Selim — fell seriously ill. The renowned physician Merkez Efendi prepared a special paste from 41 different spices and herbs, which restored her to health.

At Hafsa Sultan's command, this healing paste — mesir macunu — was to be thrown from the minarets and domes of Sultan Camisi every spring, as a gift to the people of Manisa. The tradition has been repeated without interruption for 486 years.

In 2012, UNESCO inscribed the Manisa Mesir Macunu Festival on the Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity. It is one of Turkey's most significant living cultural traditions.

The Mesir Saçım ceremony — the main event

The climax of the festival is the mesir saçımı — the scattering of macunu. Ten tonnes of individually wrapped mesir macunu packages are thrown from the minarets and domes of Sultan Camisi into the crowd below. Tens of thousands of people scramble to catch a piece — it's believed to bring health, luck and good fortune.

I watched historical footage from 100 years ago showing the same ceremony — almost identical in form to what happens today. The continuity is remarkable and genuinely moving.

💡 First-hand tip — the saçım

Get to Sultan Meydanı early — the square fills up fast. The energy when the macunu starts falling is unlike anything else. People laugh, scramble, hold up bags. Children, grandparents, everyone. It's joyful chaos. Catch one and eat it on the spot.

The cortège — colour and ceremony

The festival opens with a procession beginning at Manisa Hükümet Konağı, moving along Mustafa Kemal Paşa Caddesi and Cumhuriyet Bulvarı before reaching Sultan Camisi. The procession features:

Temsili figures of Merkez Efendi, Hafsa Sultan and her court ladies. Representations of Ottoman sultans and princes who grew up in Manisa. International delegations and dance groups. Mehter (Ottoman military band) marching the whole route.

Foreign dance troupes added a carnival energy to the cortège — it felt genuinely international, not just a local event.

The gastronomy zone — real Manisa food

The food area at Mesir is not afterthought street food. This is serious Manisa gastronomy — dishes you won't find in İstanbul restaurants, regional specialities tied to this corner of Turkey.

🍚 Don't miss — the 5,000-person pilav

One of the most impressive moments of the festival: a dev tencere (giant cauldron) of Manisa Bahar Pilavı cooked for 5,000 people simultaneously. Watching this being prepared — and then served — is worth the trip on its own.

🎨

Bonus: Frida Kahlo Exhibition — "The Diaries of Frida Kahlo"

21 Nisan – 24 Mayıs 2026

The 486th Mesir Festival opened with something unexpected: a Frida Kahlo exhibition at Fatih Sergi Salonu in Manisa. "The Diaries of Frida Kahlo" — an officially licensed exhibition by the Frida Kahlo Corporation — ran alongside the festival from April 21 to May 24.

The pairing felt surprising at first, but worked. Frida Kahlo's work — tied to identity, pain, resilience and celebration of culture — resonated with the spirit of Mesir. The exhibition was genuinely moving and well-curated. If you're visiting Manisa for the festival, it's worth adding an hour for this.

Planning both festivals in one trip

Alaçatı and Manisa are roughly 150 km apart — about 1.5–2 hours by car. Both festivals ran in the same week of April 2026, making it entirely possible to do both in a long weekend.

📅 Combined weekend plan

Day 1
Alaçatı — Arrive for the April 23 opening cortège, full festival day, end at Ilıca beach.
Day 2
İzmir overnight — Stay in İzmir, explore the city. Kordon waterfront in the evening.
Day 3
Manisa — Drive 1.5h north. Festival day, gastronomy zone, mesir saçımı ceremony if timing aligns.
Day 3+
Frida Kahlo exhibition at Fatih Sergi Salonu — worth an extra hour in Manisa.
⚠️ Getting to Manisa

Manisa is 45 km from İzmir and well connected by bus and train. Car is easiest for flexibility. The festival draws very large crowds — parking fills up quickly, so arrive early or use public transport from İzmir.
